Static & Then Moving: Scenario 5

In this scenario the Pedestrian will initially be stopped at the position (35,0) and have a delay of 1.5 seconds before moving and ending with a final speed of 10 kilometers per hour. This will leave the pedestrian directly in the vehicle's path for 1.5 seconds. The pedestrian will continue moving until it is out of the path of the autonomous vehicle. The PCAS will react to this by slowing down and stopping 1.5m from the pedestrian. It will stay stopped until the pedestrian is no longer in the collision zone. Once the pedestrian is no longer in the path of the autonomous vehicle the PCAS shall no longer notify the Vehicle Brake by Wire System to stop the car and will allow the car to accelerate at a steady rate of 0.25g, accelerating at 2.45m/s, until the autonomous vehicle reaches its steady state speed of 50kph. This shall happen within 5s of the pedestrian no longer being in the path of the autonomous vehicle.
