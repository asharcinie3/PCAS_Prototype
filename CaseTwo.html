Static & Then Moving: Scenario 5

In this scenario, the Pedestrian will initially be stopped at the position (35,0), which is in the collision zone. They will have a delay of 1.5 seconds before moving and ending with a final speed of 10 kilometers per hour(2.77 m/s). This will leave the pedestrian directly in the vehicle's path for 1.86 seconds. The vehicle will already be moving at a steady rate velocity of 50km/h (13.89m/s).

Location: Delay before moving: Speed:
Pedestrian (35, 0) 1.5 s 2.77 m/s
Vehicle (0, 0) NA 13.89 m/s
Pedestrian Collision:
Duration in Zone: 1.86 s
Collision Avoided? yes

In each scenario in case two, the PCAS will input the data from the stereo camera and determine that it needs to decelerate as the system will not exceed 16km/h (4.4m/s) if a pedestrian is within 4.5m away. It will then come to a stop, 1.5m from the pedestrian, while the pedestrian is in the collisin zone. It will stay stopped until the pedestrian is no longer in the collision zone. Once the pedestrian is no longer in the path of the autonomous vehicle the PCAS shall no longer notify the Vehicle Brake by Wire System to stop the car. The car will then accelerate at a steady rate of 0.25g, accelerating at 2.45m/s, until the autonomous vehicle reaches its steady state speed of 50kph(13.89 m/s). This shall happen within 5 seconds of the pedestrian no longer being in the path of the autonomous vehicle.
